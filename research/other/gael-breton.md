## 1. Affiliate Marketing Strategies Publishers Should Consider
- **Type:** Opinion / Strategy Article
- **Source:** State of Digital Publishing (SODP)
- **Link:** https://www.stateofdigitalpublishing.com/opinion/affiliate-marketing-opportunities/
- **Summary:**
Updated on December 1, 2025, and created by **Gael Breton** (with fact-checking by Vahe Arabian and editing by Andrew Kemp), this article explores how digital publishers can tap into the highly undervalued channel of **affiliate marketing**. Breton highlights that while publishers traditionally rely heavily on banner ads, paywalls, and subscriptions, affiliate marketing offers a robust, performance-based monetization model that can significantly diversify and boost their revenue streams.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - **Four Core Components:** The affiliate marketing industry functions through the collaboration of four essential pillars: the merchant (the brand), the network (program manager), the publisher (the affiliate), and the customer.
    - **Smart Program Selection:** Publishers should select programs by aligning products with their target audience, evaluating commission structures to maximize return on investment (ROI), researching the merchant’s industry reputation, and carefully reading the terms and conditions.
    - **High-Value Content Integration:** Instead of publishing hard sales pitches, publishers should naturally integrate affiliate links into helpful, informative content (such as comprehensive product reviews) based on high-traffic keywords identified through tools like Google Ads' Keyword Planner.
    - **Multi-Channel Promotion:** Affiliate promotions can be optimized using platform-specific strategies, including Pinterest for visual goods, Facebook for physical/digital items, Twitter, YouTube (for video reviews and niche channels), and email newsletters.
    - **Patience and Consistency:** Affiliate marketing is not a shortcut to instant revenue, but publishers who commit time and consistent effort can build highly stable, long-term **passive income**.
- **Why it matters:** It provides digital publishers with a practical roadmap to move away from sole reliance on traditional ads and subscriptions, turning high-quality, audience-centric content into a scalable performance-based income stream.

## 2. Stop Chatting With AI. Start Delegating To It.
- **Type:** AI Automation Strategy / Course Announcement
- **Source:** Authority Hacker
- **Link:** https://www.authorityhacker.com/blog/claude-code-for-marketers-course-announcement/
- **Summary:**
Published on February 6, 2026, by Authority Hacker Co-Founder **Gael Breton**, this article introduces a practical, 15-lesson course designed to democratize **Claude Code** for non-technical marketers and business operators. Breton addresses the major gap in current AI adoption, explaining how marketers can shift from simply chatting with AI to mendelegasikan multi-step workflows directly within their local computers and applications without writing a single line of code.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - **Chat AI vs. Action AI:** Standard "Chat AI" only provides suggestions that force users to copy-paste data across dozens of open tabs. In contrast, "Action AI" (like Claude Code) reads local files, integrates with tools (such as Notion, Slack, and Google Sheets), and executes complex workflows autonomously.
    - **Permanent Brand Memory:** Claude Code can permanently retain brand guidelines, audience profiles, and style sheets across all sessions, removing the need to repeatedly re-explain business context.
    - **Reusable "Skills" Library:** Marketers can train Claude to execute highly repetitive operational processes (like competitor research, thumbnail design assistance, or newsletter drafting), save them as custom "skills," and share them across their teams to run automatically in the future.
    - **Parallel Multi-Agent Workflows:** Users can describe a high-level outcome, and Claude will spawn multiple parallel agents to research, analyze, and draft tasks autonomously in the background while the user focuses on other priorities.
    - **Proven Operational Time Savings:** Breton shares actual performance metrics from his 8-figure business, showing dramatic efficiency improvements: YouTube thumbnail variation generation (from 2–3 hours to 15 minutes), SEO content gap analysis (from 4–6 hours in Ahrefs to 2 minutes), and newsletter drafting (from 2–3 hours to 20 minutes).
- **Why it matters:** It demystifies powerful, developer-focused terminal tools for non-technical business leaders, turning AI from a passive advice-giver into an active teammate that executes daily marketing and business operations directly on local files.
