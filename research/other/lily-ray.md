## 1. How SEO & GEO Work Together - Do Great GEO Without Destroying Your SEO
- **Type:** Slide Deck / Conference Talk
- **Source:** SlideShare (from Summer Speaking Tour)
- **Link:** https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-seo-geo-work-together-do-great-geo-without-destroying-your-seo/288304832
- **Summary:**
This presentation, delivered across multiple major SEO conferences (including Athens SEO, Croatia SEO Summit, and CAMPIXX Berlin), addresses the intersection of traditional SEO and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). Ray explains why popular hacks designed to optimize for AI search often backfire, causing organic search visibility drops, which in turn leads to a decline in AI Overview and LLM citations.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - **Algorithmic Interdependency:** Because LLMs retrieve and ground their answers in top organic search results, a drop in Google rankings directly degrades a brand's AI search visibility.
    - **Spam Enforcement:** Spam teams at search engines are actively monitoring manipulated AI citations, leading to manual actions and algorithmic demotions for over-optimized sites.
    - **Defensive Strategy:** SEO is as much about what *not* to do as it is about what to do; brands must prioritize long-term search safety over short-term hacks.
- **Why it matters:** It shifts the perspective of GEO from a standalone channel to a subset of traditional organic SEO, reinforcing that technical authority and quality are the only sustainable paths to visibility.

## 2. Why Calling Yourself the "Best" Could Be Helping Your Competitors Win in AI Search
- **Type:** Substack Article
- **Source:** Lily Ray's Substack (Lily's Safe Space)
- **Link:** https://lilyraynyc.substack.com/p/why-calling-yourself-the-best-could
- **Summary:**
This article analyzes B2B "best [category]" queries in Google AI Overviews to test the effectiveness of the self-promotional listicle hack (where a brand publishes a listicle ranking themselves #1). Ray discovered that Google has decoupled citations from recommendations, leading to a scenario where a self-promotional article is cited as a source, but the AI recommends the competitors listed within it instead.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - **The Decoupling Shift:** Brands that published self-promotional listicles and got cited in AI Overviews were excluded from the actual recommendation 69% of the time. The AI cited the listicle but recommended the competitors mentioned.
    - **Consensus & Off-Page Authority:** AI engine recommendations are anchored to actual brand authority and web-wide consensus (third-party links and brand mentions) rather than self-proclaimed expertise.
    - **Algorithmic Demotions:** Google demoted many self-promotional listicle domains in early 2026 core updates, and AI Overviews have begun displaying disclaimers warning users about "saturated markets of self-proclaimed experts."
- **Why it matters:** It exposes the dangers of biased, self-referential content in AI search environments. It proves that to be recommended by LLMs, brands must focus on earning authentic off-page authority and third-party validation.
