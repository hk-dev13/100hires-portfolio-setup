## 1. AI Mode Isn’t Sticky Yet
- **Type:** Search Engine Usage Study
- **Source:** iPullRank Research
- **Link:** https://ipullrank.com/ai-mode-report-july-2025
- **Summary:**
A study analyzing SimilarWeb data from over 100,000 users during the first two months of Google's AI Mode public rollout (May 20 – July 19, 2025). The data examines user retention, query lengths, and session depth to evaluate whether users are forming habits around Google's generative search interface.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - **Low Retention/Stickiness:** 53% of users who tried AI Mode only did so once and did not return. Only ~9% became power users (utilizing it 5+ days), indicating that generative search has not yet become habitual.
    - **Query Length Divergence:** Traditional Google queries average 3 words, AI Mode queries average 7 words, and ChatGPT queries average ~70 words. Word counts in AI Mode are rising slowly, showing users are gradually learning to write longer, natural-language prompts.
    - **Session Depth Jump:** Searches per session in AI Mode rose 27% in mid-July (from 2.6 to 3.3) after Google introduced updates like "deep research" and multi-perspective summaries.
    - **Discovery Bottleneck:** AI Mode is currently hidden behind search icons, tabs, or links. To scale, Google must move it to the default interface, which they are balancing against the risk of ad revenue cannibalization.

## 2. Implications of AI Mode and Personal Context — 2025 Google I/O Hot Takes
- **Type:** Strategic Industry Analysis
- **Source:** iPullRank Strategy
- **Link:** https://ipullrank.com/ai-mode-and-personal-context-google-io
- **Summary:**
An analysis of Google I/O 2025 product releases, specifically the US rollout of AI Mode (Gemini 2.5) and the integration of "Personal Context" into Google Search. It outlines how hyper-personalization, query fan-outs, and transactional agents alter traditional SEO reporting and content strategy.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - **Query Fan-Out Mechanics:** LLMs decompose user prompts into parallel sub-queries, scraping results across websites and knowledge graphs to synthesize one summary. This is why low-ranking pages are frequently pulled into AI citations.
    - **Personal Context Integration:** Google Search is integrating personal data (Gmail inboxes, receipt scans, Photos, newsletters, location) to customize recommendations, making universal rankings obsolete.
    - **Agentic Search Transactions:** Google is shifting from informational answers to transactional execution (booking tickets, making restaurant reservations, ordering products directly). Brands must optimize websites for AI agents.
    - **SEO to Relevance Engineering:** In a personalized, zero-click landscape, reporting must shift to LLM visibility monitoring, topical authority depth, brand lift surveys, and direct/branded homepage traffic trends.
    - **Agent-Ready Optimization:** To earn recommendations from transactional agents, websites must expose structured data (schema, APIs, product feeds, clear policies) so agents can parse and complete actions on behalf of the user.
