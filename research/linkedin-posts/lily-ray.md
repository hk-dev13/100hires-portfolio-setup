## Post 1

**Published:**
2026-06-30 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lily-ray-44755615_this-years-summer-speaking-tour-has-officially-activity-7477392626749546496-N_pj

### Summary

Lily summarizes her summer speaking tour topic: "How SEO & GEO Work Together - Do Great GEO Without Destroying Your SEO". She warns that many popular GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) tactics actually backfire, hurting traditional organic SEO. Since AI language models draw their information from top organic search results, destroying your organic SEO naturally leads to a collapse in your AI search visibility.

### Key Takeaways

- **SEO & GEO Interdependence:** AI search systems feed on top-ranking organic content. If you hurt your Google organic rankings, your ChatGPT/Claude citations will fall too.
- **Backfiring Hacks:** Quick-fix GEO optimization shortcuts can trigger spam algorithms or search penalties, leading to a loss in both search engines and generative answer engines.
- **Holistic Alignment:** Marketers must align AEO/GEO initiatives with solid, standard SEO principles rather than running them as separate, manipulative channels.

### Why It Matters

This highlights the critical risk of separating GEO from SEO. For AI-assisted content workflows, it proves that we must not use aggressive AI generation hacks that could look spammy to search engines, as doing so will ultimately eliminate AI engine visibility as well.

## Post 2

**Published:**
2026-06-04 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lily-ray-44755615_the-worst-thing-you-can-do-for-your-ai-search-activity-7467554630458093568-81Xg

### Summary

Lily emphasizes the adage: "if you fall in Google, you fall in AI." She argues that many SEO/GEO hacks act as temporary loopholes that eventually lead to algorithmic drops or manual spam actions. She advises playing "defensive SEO" and aiming for long-term strategies that survive search engine spam cleanups.

### Key Takeaways

- **Loophole Penalties:** Manipulative GEO tactics have caught up with websites, leading to manual actions and crashes.
- **Defensive SEO:** Good SEO is as much about knowing what *not* to do (avoiding shortcuts that look like spam) as it is about what to do.
- **LLM Anti-Spam Measures:** Large Language Models (ChatGPT, Claude) are actively taking steps to filter out spam and manipulation from their own recommendation databases.

### Why It Matters

This post provides a strong warning against short-sighted GEO strategies. As search engines and LLM crawlers improve their spam filters, sustainable and high-quality content production is the only way to guarantee long-term citation stability.

## Post 3

**Published:**
2026-06-18 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lily-ray-44755615_for-the-last-couple-of-years-the-easiest-activity-7473139503277215746-4Dvf

### Summary

Lily presents research based on 100 B2B "best [category]" queries, showing that the common GEO hack of publishing self-promotional listicles and naming yourself #1 is now backfiring. Google's AI Overviews have decoupled citations from recommendations, often citing the self-promotional listicle as a source while recommending competitor brands.

### Key Takeaways

- **Decoupling Citations & Recommendations:** Getting cited as a source in an AI Overview does not guarantee you are recommended. For self-promotional listicles, the author brand was excluded from the recommendation 69% of the time.
- **Authority Anchoring:** Recommendations are anchored to actual brand authority and web-wide consensus (what others say about you), not what you write about yourself.
- **AI Disclaimers:** Both Google AI Overviews and Claude have introduced warnings on category searches flagging that listings are full of "self-proclaimed experts."

### Why It Matters

This is an important strategic insight for programmatic and product SEO. Writing biased, self-promotional content actually serves to promote competitors when parsed by AI engines. True authority must be built off-page and validated by third-party sources.
