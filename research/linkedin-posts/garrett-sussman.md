## Post 1

**Published:**
2026-06-30 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/garrettsussman_heres-my-seo-week-presentation-on-personalization-activity-7477362250438230017-OoBA

### Summary

Garrett shares his SEO Week presentation on the impact of personalization in AI Search (using Google Personal Intelligence and Google AI Mode). He explains that AI Search recommendations are heavily influenced not just by explicit user prompts, but by implicit/inferred personal context (such as emails in Gmail, Google Photos, receipts, newsletters, and mail scans). This layered personalization makes AI Search measurement far more complex than traditional rank tracking.

### Key Takeaways

- **The Möbius Strip of Context:** AI Search involves query context + explicit context (what the user tells the AI) + inferred context + implicit context + platform memory.
- **Hidden Signals:** Personal signals like newsletters, digital receipts, and emails (e.g. USPS Daily Digest) in Google accounts are parsed by AI Mode to influence search recommendations.
- **Audience Personalization Auditing:** Marketers must understand the digital signals surrounding their target personas (e.g., what newsletters they receive, what brands they already email) to influence AI recommendations.
- **Reporting Dilemma:** Personalization makes standard rank tracking obsolete, necessitating C-suite education and storytelling over simple directional rankings.

### Why It Matters

In modern GEO, optimizing for a generic query is no longer enough. We must build and seed brand signals across platforms (newsletters, emails, partnerships) because AI engines crawl personal data integrations to customize answers for individual users.

## Post 2

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/garrettsussman_i-honestly-dont-understand-how-you-cant-activity-7476277131975712768-OPco

### Summary

Garrett highlights the rapid growth of AI Search data and studies (covering AI visibility, shopping results, schema, and CTR curves). He cautions marketers not to make definitive decisions based on any single study, advising them instead to use the research as starting points to run custom, category-specific experiments.

### Key Takeaways

- **No Generalizations:** AI Search visibility is category-specific, model-specific, and highly personalized.
- **Smarter Testing:** Marketers should segment their own visibility data by model, audience, topic, prompt, and search intent.
- **Current Curated Studies:**
    - ChatGPT 5.5's fan-out patterns emphasize brand authority (Reynolds & Haigler).
    - ChatGPT AI Shopping citation logic (Wu - Profound).
    - AI citation behaviors and Reddit translation dominance.
    - Zero-click search curves showing less than 1/3 of Google queries send clicks (Fishkin).

### Why It Matters

As search becomes decentralized across models (Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude) and personalized for each user, standard SEO playbooks fall short. Marketers need to build a pipeline of custom experiments to track how their brand ranks for their specific category and target prompts.

## Post 3

**Published:**
2026-06-11 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/garrettsussman_you-cant-willy-nilly-your-ai-search-program-activity-7475488475789598722-HRIU

### Summary

Garrett emphasizes that AI Search is too massive and volatile to be managed casually by a single person running random prompt checks. Because visibility signals are spread across diverse platforms (SEO, PR, reviews, social, product marketing) and models change frequently, brands must establish a dedicated, cross-functional "AI Search Department" or task force.

### Key Takeaways

- **Cross-Functional Co-ordination:** AI Search strategy intersects content, SEO, digital PR, social, product marketing, video, and reviews.
- **Model Volatility & Zero-Sum Citations:** Model updates cause massive shifts (e.g. Reddit citations jumping 4.4x in one ChatGPT update). Including one brand usually displaces another.
- **The Core Strategy Questions:**
    1. Which platforms do our buyers use?
    2. Which sources feed those AI engines?
    3. What evidence makes the model choose us over competitors?
    4. How do we monitor and improve brand sentiment and reputation?

### Why It Matters

Since AI models pull from diverse digital touchpoints, content strategy must be integrated. The SEO team alone cannot win AI visibility; they must coordinate with PR (for mentions), product (for reviews), and social (for Reddit/YouTube transcripts) to build the entity graph that LLMs trust.
