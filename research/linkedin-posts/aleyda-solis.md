**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_fan-out-framework-5-steps-to-improve-seo-activity-7477376945052262400-1b5W

### Summary

Aleyda shares Cyrus Shepard's "Fan-out Framework," a 5-step process designed to convert AI fan-out queries (queries generated by AI search engines behind the scenes) into better search rankings and increased visibility within AI platforms.

### Key Takeaways

- **Fan-out Queries:** AI search systems generate synthetic queries to retrieve information; optimizing for these queries is essential for visibility.
- **The 5-Step Process:**
  1. Find a keyword topic you already rank for.
  2. Identify the associated Fan-out Queries.
  3. Determine the most critical Fan-out topics for your audience.
  4. Optimize your content for these specific Fan-out Queries.
  5. Measure and track the visibility results.

### Why It Matters

This framework provides a direct bridge between traditional keyword rankings and AI visibility. It outlines a systematic way for content creators to target the secondary "synthetic" queries that AI search engines actually use to retrieve answers.

## Post 2

**Published:**
2026-06-26 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_after-sharing-the-3-layer-framework-to-activity-7475635998383652864-Mz8q

### Summary

Aleyda outlines a practical breakdown of business impact metrics for measuring AI search visibility. She advises against reporting "AI impact" as a single blended number and recommends dividing metrics into four confidence layers: Observed, Own Proxy, Third-Party Proxy, and Modelled.

### Key Takeaways

- **Observed Metrics:** Direct measurements from known AI referrals and conversion paths.
- **Own Proxy Signals:** First-party downstream signals such as branded search, direct demand, surfaced-page demand, and search engine console data (e.g., Bing Webmaster Tools).
- **Third-Party Proxy Signals:** External estimates to benchmark traffic share and prompt presence against competitors.
- **Modelled Estimates:** Planning estimates for influenced revenue, which should be used for planning rather than concrete attribution proof.

### Why It Matters

As AI search reduces direct referral traffic, measuring SEO ROI becomes more complex. This classification provides a realistic framework for reporting AI search presence and impact to stakeholders without overclaiming or underselling.

## Post 3

**Published:**
2026-06-02 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/michaelkingphilly_quick-tip-the-case-for-cloaking-for-large-activity-7460696588773134336-IzBn

### Summary

Aleyda discusses research from Similarweb and Rand Fishkin on the downstream impact of AI visibility. She highlights that AI-influenced demand often arrives through Direct or Organic Search rather than direct AI referral links, making traditional attribution models insufficient.

### Key Takeaways

- **ChatGPT Influence:** Brands recommended by ChatGPT are 2.5x more likely to receive a visit within 7 days compared to non-recommended competitors.
- **Search as Downstream Channel:** 55.9% of AI-influenced visits come via search engines because users search for the recommended brand later.
- **SERP Protection:** Branded SERPs (and brand comparison queries) must be protected and aligned with AI answers because users return to search to verify.
- **Higher Engagement:** AI-influenced visitors engage more deeply, viewing twice as many pages and staying twice as long.

### Why It Matters

This research reveals that AI search functions as a brand-discovery engine. Even without a direct link click from an AI Overview or ChatGPT, being cited in AI responses drives significant organic traffic. Marketers must optimize branded search terms to capture this downstream demand.
