## Post 1

**Published:**
2026-07-01 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_the-bar-to-beat-when-it-comes-to-primary-activity-7477700063385989120-2JlX

### Summary

Kevin explains that the barrier to entry for ranking with primary research is lower than many assume. Citing a study by OnPage AI that analyzed 150 top-3 Google rankings across 50 keywords, he notes that original data is the single best predictor of an information gain score. The average top ranking page only has about 4 unique data points, meaning that publishing 5 or more original figures immediately places a page ahead of the competition.

### Key Takeaways

- **Original Data is King:** Incorporating original data points is a better predictor of high information gain scores than word count.
- **Low Industry Bar:** The average top-performing organic result features only 4 unique data points.
- **Figures Scale Information Gain:** Pages with 1 or fewer data points score 40.2 in information gain, while those with 15 or more score 62.1.

### Why It Matters

In AI-assisted content creation, this shows that we must steer clear of simple rewrites. To achieve high search rankings and AI visibility, content pipelines should prioritize generating and embedding unique statistics, surveys, or proprietary data points rather than merely lengthening text.

## Post 2

**Published:**
2026-06-30 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_publishing-data-only-your-product-can-produce-activity-7477372003444789248-D5Ev

### Summary

Kevin highlights that the most defensible AI citation asset a business has is proprietary data generated as a byproduct of its own product or operations. However, he warns that owning the data is not enough; sellers must also structure the data to be easily extracted by LLMs and establish general topical trust for the site.

### Key Takeaways

- **Defensibility of Proprietary Data:** Competitors cannot easily copy or replicate statistics generated directly by your product's telemetry or operations.
- **Three Pillars of AI Citation:**
    1. The proprietary data itself.
    2. Proper data structuring (making it easy for LLMs to extract in a single pass).
    3. Site-wide topical authority and trust.
- **Data Readability:** Original data must be clear and structured so AI crawlers can lift and parse the statistics in one read.

### Why It Matters

When optimizing content for AI Search engines (like Gemini or ChatGPT Search), we must structure our proprietary data points clearly (e.g. using tables, bullet lists, bold text, or JSON-LD) so AI crawlers can easily extract and cite them.

## Post 3

**Published:**
2026-06-25 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_two-brands-can-run-identical-on-page-optimization-activity-7473336241837731841-Dl3f

### Summary

Kevin discusses the gap in AI citation rates between brands with identical on-page SEO. He explains that AI engines reuse existing brand trust and entity recognition from third-party mentions (such as media publications, analyst reports, and competitors) rather than relying solely on a site's own claims of authority.

### Key Takeaways

- **Off-Property Reputation:** AI search models rely heavily on existing trust linked to recognized entities across the web.
- **Limits of Self-Proclaimed Trust:** A site's own blog is a weak authority input for LLMs since the model has no baseline reason to trust self-published claims.
- **Third-Party Validation:** Citations and mentions from independent third parties (analysts, media, competitors) build the baseline trust that AI search models look for.

### Why It Matters

This shifts the focus of modern SEO from pure on-page tuning (e.g. titles, meta tags) to digital PR and entity building. To win AI Overviews and chatbot citations, brands must build off-page authority through press mentions and third-party references.
