## Post 1

**Published:**
2025-07-02 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/gael-breton_your-business-website-costs-5k-and-6-weeks-activity-7452952555195682816-Fgro

### Summary

Gael outlines the arguments for building static websites (using frameworks like Astro) instead of stacking plugins on expensive WordPress sites. He details how static sites naturally achieve Lighthouse scores of 95+ on mobile and have superior semantic HTML and accessibility. He suggests Git, DecapCMS, or instructing Claude Code directly as viable ways for clients to edit content.

### Key Takeaways

- **Static vs. WordPress:** Static frameworks like Astro produce cleaner, more semantic HTML, which is optimal for both Lighthouse page speed metrics and AI engine readability.
- **AI-Driven CMS Workflows:** Instead of complex CMS dashboards, non-technical clients can use Claude Code to make site changes by simply describing edits in plain English.
- **Decoupling Functionality:** SaaS script tags (HubSpot, Calendly, Plausible) make it easy to integrate bookings, CRM, and analytics on static sites without needing a heavy WordPress plugin database.

### Why It Matters

In programmatic SEO, site architecture and speed are critical. This post highlights that migrating to static sites (Astro) combined with Claude Code is a cheaper, faster, and more SEO/GEO-friendly approach than maintaining bloated WordPress stacks.

## Post 2

**Published:**
2026-05-02 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/gael-breton_your-ai-articles-read-fine-but-say-nothing-activity-7452295174195507200-4ks2

### Summary

Gael critiques the current state of AI-generated articles that merely scrape and rewrite the top 10 Google search results, yielding "zero information gain." To solve this, he recommends using AI agents to research real human experiences on YouTube, Reddit, and Twitter, and structuring content section-by-section using Claude.

### Key Takeaways

- **Information Gain Deficiency:** Grammatically perfect AI articles are useless if they only recycle the top 10 search results. Research depth, not better writing, is the key differentiator.
- **The Frustrated Reader Prompt:** Use a sub-agent to analyze existing Google results and find intent gaps ("what remains unanswered?").
- **Off-Google Research:** Direct research agents to scrape Reddit, Twitter, and YouTube where people share authentic, lived experiences (e.g., finding a "2-campaign loop" Meta ads strategy on Reddit).

### Why It Matters

This is a key methodology for AI-assisted content workflows. To avoid search penalties and satisfy E-E-A-T guidelines, AI content pipelines must integrate web-scraping agents that dig into community forums rather than just rewriting SERP competitors.

## Post 3

**Published:**
2026-03-02 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/gael-breton_everyone-assumes-chromes-new-ai-features-activity-7424768787989688321-HmCa

### Summary

Gael argues that Chrome's side-panel AI features (context on the left, AI chat on the right) will not kill websites. Instead, it will encourage users to open sites to check trust signals (design, load speed) and fact-check AI outputs, a layer of subtext that is missing in sanitized chatbot interfaces.

### Key Takeaways

- **Side-Panel Search:** Browser-based AI models encourage users to keep websites open while engaging with AI, rather than isolating search inside closed chatbot windows.
- **Design as a Trust Signal:** A website's appearance, speed, and build act as crucial visual authority markers for users evaluating the credibility of the cited author.
- **Chatbot Limitations:** Clean, text-only chatbot responses strip away the brand-specific context and trust indicators that a full website interface provides.

### Why It Matters

This emphasizes that even as AI search adoption grows, on-page user experience and branding remain vital. Brands must invest in high-quality design and performance (Lighthouse scores) because users will still open sites to verify the credibility of AI recommendations.
