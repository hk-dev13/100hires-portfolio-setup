## Post 1

**Published:**
2026-07-01 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosshudgens_the-average-age-of-a-youtube-video-appearing-activity-7477748118806265856-p2UU

### Summary

Ross shares research on B2B and B2C YouTube videos appearing in transactional "best X" search prompts. They analyzed 366 videos and found the average age of a video appearing in these prompts is 9.1 months (12.5 months in B2C, 7.7 months in B2B). These videos are mostly produced by affiliates and third-party partners. Ross suggests this represents an arbitrage opportunity for brands currently ignoring video SEO and details the monetization shifting to upfront creator payments ($6,000–$10,000) plus conversion incentives.

### Key Takeaways

- **Video Longevity in AI Search:** YouTube videos remain visible in transactional generative search queries for nearly a year on average.
- **Affiliate/Partner Dominance:** Third-party reviews and affiliate content dominate video citations in "best X" queries.
- **Arbitrage Opportunity:** Many SEO practitioners ignore video integration, leaving a brief window to leverage visual search and transcripts for AI Visibility.
- **Creator Cost Shift:** High-quality video creators now demand upfront payments ($6k–$10k) plus commission incentives, reflecting low CTR but high-influence value.

### Why It Matters

This shows that transcripts and video files are essential sources of grounding data for AI engines. Brands should invest in video marketing and influencer relations to secure conversational product recommendations that are frequently cited in AIOs.

## Post 2

**Published:**
2026-06-18 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosshudgens_b2c-content-marketing-does-not-drive-many-activity-7472660098964959232-Hc4I

### Summary

Ross discusses B2C vs B2B click-through rates from LLMs. Siege Media's study of 61 brands showed B2C editorial/blog pages received only 7% of LLM clicks, whereas B2B editorial pages received over 25%. This is because B2B has high-intent bottom-of-funnel (BOFU) comparison and alternative pages that convert well, while B2C relies on affiliates or product pages for these queries.

### Key Takeaways

- **B2C Blog Traffic Low in LLMs:** B2C editorial blogs get very little click-through value (7%) from AI chatbot search engines.
- **B2B BOFU Strength:** B2B comparison keywords ("Best X", "X vs Y Alternatives") drive strong citations and clicks to brand blogs.
- **B2C LLM Strategy Shift:** For B2C, LLM influence is driven through product page optimization, overall site authority, and third-party affiliate mentions rather than informational blogs.

### Why It Matters

It provides a clear strategy divide. B2B content pipelines should double-down on commercial comparison articles ("Alternatives to X"), while B2C brands should focus on optimizing e-commerce product pages and building digital PR backlinks on affiliate sites that AI engines scrape.

## Post 3

**Published:**
2026-06-11 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosshudgens_ai-traffic-converts-34-better-than-organic-activity-7470498245148934144-JQb4

### Summary

Ross shares that AI search engine traffic converts 34% better than standard organic traffic on average (49% in B2B, 19% in B2C), based on a study of 77 sites. He dispels the early hype of 4x-7x conversion multipliers but stresses that LLM-referred users represent high-intent buyers, particularly due to B2B bottom-of-funnel comparison content.

### Key Takeaways

- **AI Intent Premium:** Traffic referred by LLM engines converts 34% better than traditional Google organic search.
- **B2B vs B2C Conversion Gap:** B2B AI traffic converts 49% better, while B2C converts 19% better, driven by bottom-funnel comparison queries.
- **Content Justification:** Having comparison content (e.g. "X vs Y", "X alternatives") is necessary to capture this high-converting traffic.
- **Measurement Gaps:** Approximately 25% of Google Analytics profiles analyzed lacked proper goal and conversion tracking setups.

### Why It Matters

This data justifies the resources required to build and maintain commercial comparison page catalogs. Without structured comparison content, brands fail to capture the high-converting buyer traffic referred by AI models.
