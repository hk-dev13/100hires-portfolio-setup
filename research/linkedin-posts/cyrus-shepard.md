## Post 1

**Published:**
2026-06-30 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cyrusshepard_heres-a-question-ive-seen-a-few-times-activity-7477489157133451264-KQny

### Summary

Cyrus answers a common question regarding AI fan-out queries: Should you create a new page of content for every single fan-out query? He firmly recommends against mass-producing thin, new pages, stating that it's far more effective to update existing high-ranking pages to cover intent gaps instead.

### Key Takeaways

- **Avoid AI-Generated Page Spam:** Mass-producing thin, AI-generated pages for every fan-out query will trigger Google spam demotions.
- **AI Prefers High Rankings:** AI search engines ground and cite highly ranked organic URLs. If a new page has thin content and fails to rank organically, AI engines will never see it.
- **Update Existing URLs:** In 80-90% of cases, the optimal move is to update an existing, high-ranking page to fill in the missing fan-out query intent.
- **When to Create New Pages:** Only create new pages when the query intent is unique, the query has independent search volume, and you can provide valuable, first-party experience/information.

### Why It Matters

This post provides crucial operational guidance for AI-assisted SEO workflows. It confirms that the best approach for AI search visibility is content *consolidation and enhancement* (updating existing authority pages) rather than content *proliferation* (creating hundreds of new thin pages).

### Summary

Cyrus shares a satirical post about a "Google Traffic Simulator" tool he built that generates fake Google Search Console (GSC) screenshots with custom traffic levels. While humorous, the post delivers a serious warning: do not trust every screenshot shared on social media by "gurus" claiming massive, overnight traffic gains from AI content scaling or recoveries.

### Key Takeaways

- **Social Media Skepticism:** GSC screenshots are easily faked or manipulated, and should not be used as absolute proof of success.
- **Transparency:** The SEO industry is plagued by fabricated "case studies" showing explosive traffic growth. Always demand robust, verifiable data.

### Why It Matters

In an era where many creators brag about scaling AI content to millions of pageviews overnight, this post is a reminder to remain skeptical. Authenticity, manual audit rigor, and first-party verification are vital for real-world portfolios.

## Post 3

**Published:**
2026-06-26 (Approximate)

**Source:**
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cyrusshepard_new-fan-out-framework-5-steps-to-improve-activity-7475981247811579904-oYW7

### Summary

Cyrus introduces the "Fan-out Framework," a 5-step process designed to optimize content for AI query fan-outs (the sub-queries generated in parallel by AI search engines to answer a user prompt). 

### Key Takeaways

- **Optimizing for AI Sub-queries:** Achieving high visibility in AI responses requires ranking for the associated sub-queries generated behind the scenes.
- **The 5-Step Process:**
  1. Identify a keyword you already rank for.
  2. Uncover common fan-out queries related to it.
  3. Determine the most important fan-out topics to address.
  4. Optimize your existing page (or create new ones, if necessary).
  5. Measure and track the results.

### Why It Matters

This post officially introduces the "Fan-out Framework," which serves as a core workflow for GEO. It shifts the standard SEO content process from keyword targeting to mapping out clusters of machine-generated intent blocks.
