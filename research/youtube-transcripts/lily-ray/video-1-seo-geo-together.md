## Executive Summary

In this interview with Danny Goodwin (Search Engine Land), Lily Ray shares her origin story in SEO, from Craigslist jobs in the Bay Area to VP of SEO & AI Search at Amsive and founder of Algorythmic. She discusses her early experience with Penguin penalties and explains how the 2018 Medic update pivoted her focus toward E-E-A-T. She argues that search quality principles are fundamental to how AI platforms are starting to evaluate sources and filter out spam.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **E-E-A-T is a Long-Term Concept:** Google's emphasis on E-E-A-T (Expertise, Experience, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) is a model designed to emulate quality, and these principles are now being adapted by LLMs to combat AI spam.
- **Attributing AI Visibility:** AI search platforms (like ChatGPT) rely on search engines to discover URLs. Therefore, organic SEO rankings directly fuel your AI citation visibility.
- **Risk-Averse SEO:** Experiencing algorithmic drops (like Penguin or Medic) highlights the importance of "defensive SEO"—focusing on clean, sustainable fundamentals rather than temporary hacks that eventually get penalized as spam.
