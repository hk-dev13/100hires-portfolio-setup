## Why This Video Was Selected

This video is selected because it outlines a highly actionable, technical case study of "Lean SEO" at scale on Moz.com. It details crawl budget optimization, duplicate content pruning, and internal link routing, which are foundational technical concepts.

---

## Executive Summary

Britney Muller details her first year as the in-house SEO at Moz.com. She outlines five major "secrets" (technical case studies) that moved the needle: pruning 70,000 thin, spammy community profile pages (reducing indexed profiles by 98%), configuring over 1 million URL parameters in Search Console to optimize crawl budgets, migrating the Learn Center to a modern CMS (Craft CMS) for crawlability and schema, improving recommended related articles to increase pages per session, and educating non-SEO employees through hands-on optimization.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Crap Pruning (Importance Optimization):** Pruning thin, low-traffic, or spammy pages (like inactive forum profiles) helps consolidate domain authority and directs search crawler attention to high-value pages.
- **Crawl Budget and Parameters:** Configuring URL parameters in Search Console resolves duplicate content issues and optimizes crawl budgets for large sites.
- **Nurturing User Journeys:** Improving suggested related content links at the bottom of pages directly increases pages per session and overall user engagement.
- **In-House SEO Education:** Helping non-SEO co-workers learn by doing (e.g. creating pages to rank for silly keywords) builds a culture of search awareness and reduces technical debt.
