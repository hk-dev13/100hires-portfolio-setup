## Executive Summary

In this comprehensive presentation, Michael King controversially declares that "SEO is deprecated" and must evolve into "Relevance Engineering"—the confluence of AI, content strategy, digital PR, UX, and information retrieval. He breaks down how AI Overviews and LLMs are drastically changing user behavior (fewer clicks but higher quality conversions) and shifts the underlying search mechanism from deterministic to probabilistic (Agentic RAG). He argues that relying on traditional SEO checklists is a failing strategy.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **SEO is Deprecated:** The traditional "checklist culture" of SEO no longer works. The industry is moving to Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) and Relevance Engineering, requiring statistical rigor and a deep understanding of information retrieval.
- **Probabilistic vs. Deterministic Search:** Traditional lexical search (TF-IDF/BM25) is deterministic, while modern AI search using LLMs and embeddings is probabilistic (content is reasoned upon on every load).
- **Traffic Shifts:** AI Overviews are causing significant traffic drops (fewer clicks, less pogo-sticking), but the traffic that does click through tends to be highly qualified with better conversion rates.
- **Google's Enduring Advantage:** Despite the rise of Perplexity and ChatGPT, Google is likely to win the AI search war due to its proprietary data pipeline, custom chips, and massively distributed consumer products.
