## Why This Video Was Selected

This video was selected because speaking at conferences and participating in the wider SEO community is a key digital PR activity that builds authority. Understanding how major event organizers select topics provides insights into the latest, most valued concepts in technical and AI SEO.

---

## Executive Summary

In this episode of Crawling Mondays, Aleyda Solís is joined by Kelvin Newman (BrightonSEO) and Kirsty Holkin (International Search Summit) to discuss the ins and outs of pitching to speak at major digital marketing events. They cover common mistakes, the selection criteria organizers use, how they manage slots, and actionable advice for first-time speakers looking to break into the industry.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Don't Take Rejection Personally:** Event organizers turn down many excellent pitches simply due to slot limitations, topic overlap, or scheduling constraints.
- **Be Specific and Actionable:** Formulating talks around specific problems ("How to do X in Y") is much more successful than broad, generic topics (e.g., "Intro to Link Building").
- **Avoid Self-Promotion:** Organizers easily detect pitches that are disguised sales pitches for a brand or service; focus on delivering value first.
- **Speaker Diversity and First-Timers:** Organizers actively seek to support new and underrepresented speakers, looking at speaker potential rather than just past experience.
