## Executive Summary

Cyrus Shepard joins host Chris Raulf (Boulder SEO Marketing) to discuss his comprehensive meta-analysis of 54 studies on AI citations. Cyrus outlines that organic Google rankings are the single largest predictor of appearing in AI search engines since LLMs rely heavily on search results for grounding. They also discuss how webmasters can inadvertently block AI bots, the mechanics of query fan-outs, and why specific factual density outperforms prose in AI search environments.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **SEO Rankings Drive AI Citations:** Ranking in the top 10 of Google is the single strongest factor for being cited in AI search engines (like ChatGPT or Google AI Overviews), which draw heavily on search results for grounding.
- **AI Bot Accessibility is Essential:** Many sites inadvertently block AI crawlers via robots.txt or Cloudflare settings. Ensuring crawlability is a prerequisite for GEO.
- **The Power of Query Fan-out:** AI engines break down prompts into multiple sub-queries. Ranking for multiple related sub-queries significantly multiplies the probability of being cited.
- **Factual Density over Prose:** AI engines reward specific, structured data (like tables, lists, and direct Q&As) because they serve as direct evidence to support the AI's generated response.
