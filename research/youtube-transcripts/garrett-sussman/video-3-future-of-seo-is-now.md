## Executive Summary

Jon and Joe interview Garrett Sussman about his role as Director of Marketing at iPullRank and the launch of SEO Week. Garrett explains that client education is the hardest part of modern SEO because terms like AEO, GEO, and "relevance engineering" are still fluid. He talks about how iPullRank defines Relevance Engineering as a holistic discipline comprising information retrieval, AI/RAG optimization, content strategy, digital PR, and user experience. He discusses the problem of vector embeddings inheriting human cultural biases (e.g. gender bias in professions) and highlights the inevitability of hyper-personalized search bubbles (Google integrating Gmail, Photos, receipts to customize search). He notes that while LLMs reduce search traffic, search intent persists, forcing marketers to optimize for the whole digital footprint.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Relevance Engineering Definition:** iPullRank's term for the post-SEO era. It includes:
  1. *Information Retrieval:* Making pages technically crawlable and indexable.
  2. *AI/RAG Optimization:* Optimizing for probabilistic LLM retrieval.
  3. *Content Strategy:* Allocating resources to the right channels.
  4. *Digital PR:* Getting trusted backlinks and brand mentions off-site.
  5. *User Experience:* Ensuring smooth conversion funnels.
- **Embedding Bias Risk:** Vector databases translate words into mathematical spatial coordinates, which can inherit societal biases. Marketers must audit how their brand terms position in coordinate space.
- **Hyper-Personalization Layers:** Google is building personal search graphs using personal inboxes, receipts, and photos, creating distinct personalized search realities.
- **Omnichannel Footprint:** In the AI search age, SEOs must operate as omnichannel brand orchestrators.
