This video explains why traditional rankings do not guarantee AI search recommendations and outlines iPullRank's three-tiered AEO measurement framework and "surround sound" GEO strategy.

---

## Executive Summary

Anthony (Adsy) interviews Garrett Sussman (iPullRank) on why AI search engines ignore certain brands. Garrett explains that rankings are no longer a simple indicator of visibility because AI search is probabilistic and personalized. Instead of keyword-level rankings, visibility is measured in three tiers: input metrics (publishing velocity, bot crawl rate, mathematical relevance), channel metrics (recommendations, share of voice, citation rate), and performance metrics (traffic, conversion, revenue). He highlights the "sycophantic" nature of conversational search (it tells the user what they want to hear based on the bias of their query). In 2026, SEO requires optimizing for context, authority, and citation eligibility. To get cited in AI engines, brands must build "surround sound SEO" by getting mentioned on third-party channels (Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn, industry blogs) because AI engines scrape these sites to formulate answers.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Invisible Rankings:** Ranking #1 in a traditional SERP does not guarantee that an LLM will recommend or cite your brand in its generative output.
- **Sycophantic Chatbots:** LLMs display sycophancy (answering to confirm the user's bias). Optimizing content needs to map directly to how different audience cohorts frame questions.
- **Three-Tiered AEO Measurement:**
  1. *Input Metrics:* Math relevance, bot crawl activity, content output velocity.
  2. *Channel Metrics:* LLM share of voice (recommendations) and citation rate.
  3. *Performance Metrics:* Direct traffic, conversions, revenue.
- **Surround Sound GEO:** Winning AI search is about off-site brand authority. You must get cited on the sites LLMs scrape (Reddit, LinkedIn, YouTube, niche directories) to be recommended.
