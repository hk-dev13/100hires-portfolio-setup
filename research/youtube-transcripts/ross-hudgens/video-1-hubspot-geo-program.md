## Executive Summary

Ross Hudgens interviews Aja Frost about HubSpot's category-leading AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) / GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) strategy. HubSpot started investing in AEO in early 2023 when they saw educational traffic for basic terms ("what is content marketing") declining due to LLMs intercepting zero-click searches. To adapt, they pivoted their content strategy from pure educational traffic to driving brand mentions and authority inside LLMs (focusing on "B2B2C" — Business to Bot to Consumer). They treat LLMs as the primary audience, optimizing for page speed and parseability. Crucially, they write "TOFU with a twist," meaning they explicitly state how HubSpot's product solves the problem within informational articles, which prompts the LLM to recommend HubSpot as a solution rather than just summarizing the topic anonymously. They also leverage paid creator campaigns on LinkedIn, YouTube (optimizing transcripts and titles), and Reddit to improve citation share, and use a proprietary "citation ROI" bidding model for affiliate outreach.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **B2B2C (Business to Bot to Consumer):** Treating LLMs/agents as the primary audience by optimizing content structure and parseability.
- **TOFU with a Twist:** When writing informational content, explicitly connect the topic to your product/service features so LLMs cite and recommend your brand rather than summarizing the topic anonymously.
- **LLM/AEO Channels:** The top three platforms that LLMs scrape for citation seed sets are LinkedIn, YouTube, and Reddit. Brand mentions on these sites directly impact LLM visibility.
- **YouTube Transcript SEO:** In AI search, the video itself isn't watched; the LLM parses the video transcript. Optimizing what is said (the transcript) and metadata is critical.
- **Citation Value Bidding:** Assessing the specific monetary ROI of an LLM citation to adjust affiliate program payouts and placement strategies.
